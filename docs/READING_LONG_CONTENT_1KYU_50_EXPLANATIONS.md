# 英検1級 長文の内容一致選択 問題・解説集

本ドキュメントは `seed_reading_long_content_1kyu_50.sql` に登録された長文内容一致問題（50問）の解説です。

## 英検1級 長文の内容一致のレベル・出題傾向

### 形式（本番試験）

- **大問3**: 内容一致選択問題 … **全7問**
- **問題文A**: 約**500語**の長文・**3問**
- **問題文B**: 約**800語**の長文・**4問**
- 目標解答時間：問題文A 約12分、問題文B 約15分（計25〜30分）

### レベル

- **対象**: 大学上級程度（CEFR C1、約10,000語レベル）
- **語彙・文法**: 政治・経済・環境・自然科学・医学・テクノロジー・文化・歴史・教育・心理など、アカデミックで抽象度の高い論説文。接続表現（nevertheless, therefore, in other words など）の正確な理解が重要。
- **パッセージ語数**: 約500〜800語。段落ごとの論点・論者名・具体例を押さえた上で、設問の「段落指定」「誰の見解か」「何を illustrate しているか」に正確に答える力が求められる。

### 出題傾向のポイント

| タイプ | 内容 | 問い方の傾向 |
|--------|------|----------------|
| **段落・論者指定** | 特定段落や論者の主張に基づく設問 | **According to the first paragraph, ...** / **What does X believe?** / **According to historian Y, ...** |
| **例の役割** | 具体例が何を説明・例示しているか | **The example of X illustrates that ...** / **The author mentions X as an example that ...** |
| **作者の解釈・含意** | 筆者の解釈や言外の意味 | **How does the author interpret X's comment?** / **What does the author imply about ...?** |
| **懸念・批判** | 本文で触れられている懸念や批判 | **What is one concern the author cites with regard to ...?** |

- **テーマ**: 政治・経済・環境・自然科学・医学・テクノロジー・文化・歴史・教育・心理・社会生活。特に文化・歴史が最後の長文で扱われる傾向。
- **選択肢**: 長く複雑な文が多く、本文の言い換え・論者と内容の対応・因果の正確な一致が求められる。

## Passage 1: The Speed of Cultural Change

**問1** According to the first paragraph, what has challenged the traditional view of cultural change?  
**正解**: Fieldwork and historical analysis have shown that cultural traits can spread rapidly under favorable conditions.  
**解説**: 第1段落の "In recent decades, however, fieldwork and historical analysis have challenged this assumption. Scholars now recognize that cultural traits can spread rapidly when conditions favor them" が根拠です。

**問2** What is one defense of the gradualist view mentioned in the passage?  
**正解**: The archaeological and historical record is incomplete, so small-scale changes may leave little trace.  
**解説**: "One defense of the gradualist view has been that the archaeological and historical record is incomplete. Small-scale or short-lived changes may leave little trace" が根拠です。

**問3** What does the passage say about Susan Reynolds's argument?  
**正解**: She argued that the Norman Conquest brought about a relatively swift transformation in law, landholding, and language at the elite level.  
**解説**: "Historian Susan Reynolds has argued that the Norman Conquest of England in 1066 brought about a relatively swift transformation in law, landholding, and language at the elite level" が根拠です。

**問4** According to the passage, what is "cultural lag"?  
**正解**: The delay between the adoption of a new technology and the adjustment of social norms and institutions to it.  
**解説**: "Economists and sociologists have used the term 'cultural lag' to describe the delay between the adoption of a new technology and the adjustment of social norms and institutions to it" が根拠です。

**問5** The example of the introduction of the horse to the Americas illustrates that  
**正解**: under the right circumstances, cultural change can be both rapid and profound.  
**解説**: "Similarly, the introduction of the horse to the Americas... altered the way of life of many Indigenous peoples within a few decades. Such examples suggest that under the right circumstances, cultural change can be both rapid and profound" が根拠です。

**正解**: Minoan society was peaceful, prosperous, and dominated by a priestly or royal elite.  
**解説**: "Early excavations... led scholars to portray Minoan society as peaceful, prosperous, and dominated by a priestly or royal elite" が根拠です。

**解説**: "The collapse of Minoan civilization remains a subject of debate. The eruption of the Thera volcano... is often cited as a contributing factor... Invasion by Mycenaean Greeks from the mainland may have delivered a final blow" が根拠です。

**問10** The author of the passage suggests that

## Passage 3: Algorithmic Bias

**問11** In the first paragraph, the author illustrates the problem of algorithmic bias by mentioning cases where  
**正解**: facial recognition has misidentified people of colour more often, and job-screening algorithms have favoured candidates who mirror historically successful demographics.  
**解説**: 第1段落の "facial recognition software has been shown to misidentify people of colour more often than white people, and where algorithms used to screen job applicants have inadvertently favoured candidates who mirror the demographics of historically successful employees" が根拠です。

**問12** What is one concern the author cites with regard to companies in the "digital afterlife" industry?  
**正解**: The avatars they create may not accurately reflect the personality of the deceased, and consent is frequently unclear.  
**解説**: "ethicists point out that the avatars they create may not accurately reflect the personality or wishes of the deceased, and that consent—whether the deceased would have agreed to such use of their data—is frequently unclear" が根拠です。

**問13** According to the passage, why do biased outcomes often arise in algorithms?  
**正解**: Because the data used to train the systems reflect past discrimination or underrepresentation.  
**解説**: "they often arise because the data used to train the systems reflect past discrimination or underrepresentation. If the data are biased, the algorithm will learn and perpetuate that bias" が根拠です。

**問14** What do some researchers argue can mitigate algorithmic bias?  
**正解**: Transparency and oversight, such as auditing algorithms for fairness.  
**解説**: "Some researchers argue that transparency and oversight can mitigate algorithmic bias. Requiring that certain high-stakes algorithms be audited for fairness, or that their logic be explainable" が根拠です。

**問15** The author of the passage implies that  
**正解**: others caution that technical fixes are insufficient without broader social change.  
**解説**: "Others caution that technical fixes are insufficient without broader social change; if the underlying data and the institutions that use them remain biased, even a 'fair' algorithm may produce unfair outcomes" が根拠です。

**問50** The example of the scientific revolution illustrates that
